### Orientation

My interest in finance is grounded in the same mental model as my research: structured analysis under incomplete information, rigorous stress-testing of assumptions, and clear frameworks for decisions where the cost of being wrong is asymmetric.

### Project: Capital Allocation Strategies across Domestic and Global Markets
*Aug 2024 – Dec 2024*

Designed a quantitative decision framework for capital allocation strategies under varying risk appetites.

- Evaluated minimum-risk and Sharpe-optimal portfolios; benchmarked across domestic and global asset classes
- Analyzed risk-return trade-offs, volatility regimes, diversification benefits, and sector concentration
- Constructed and compared portfolio frontiers under different constraint regimes

### Areas of Active Interest

#### Public Markets

Bottom-up equity research, particularly in capital-intensive industries where understanding the underlying physical or operational process provides an analytical edge. The goal is to identify where consensus is structurally wrong, not just different.

#### Risk & Uncertainty

The methodologies I use in cosmological inference — uncertainty quantification, bias identification, benchmarking across competing models — transfer directly to financial modeling. Most valuation frameworks overstate the precision of their outputs.

#### Macro Frameworks

Credit cycles, monetary transmission mechanisms, and the interplay between fiscal policy and market structure. Macro as a risk management tool rather than a return driver.
