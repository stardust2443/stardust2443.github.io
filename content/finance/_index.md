### Orientation

My interest in finance is anchored in the same mental model as my research: structured analysis under incomplete information, rigorous stress-testing of assumptions, and clear frameworks for decisions where the cost of being wrong is asymmetric.

The minor in Finance at BITS Pilani provided formal grounding in valuation, corporate finance, and market structure. What I work on independently goes further.

---

### Areas of Active Interest

#### Public Markets

Bottom-up equity research, particularly in capital-intensive industries and businesses where understanding the underlying physical or operational process provides an analytical edge most participants lack. The goal is to identify where consensus is wrong, not just different.

#### Risk & Uncertainty

The methodologies I use in cosmological inference — uncertainty quantification, bias identification, benchmarking across competing models — transfer directly to financial modeling. Most valuation frameworks underestimate structural uncertainty and overstate the precision of their outputs.

#### Macro Frameworks

Credit cycles, monetary transmission mechanisms, and the interplay between fiscal policy and market structure. Macro as a risk management tool, not a return driver.

### Thesis → Finance Transfer

The void-finding research sharpened several instincts that translate to financial analysis:

> **Tracer bias in data** ↔ survivorship bias in market datasets  
> **Void size function robustness** ↔ stress-testing assumptions in valuation models  
> **Lagrangian displacement fields** ↔ mean-reversion and trajectory analysis  
> **Geometric vs. dynamical void-finders** ↔ rules-based vs. model-driven factor investing

The common thread: *what is the underlying signal, and how much does my measurement method distort it?*
